Grocery Store Chemical Toolkit

Every chemical used across all four phases of Albu, sourced from grocery stores, pharmacies, hardware stores, and Amazon. No specialty lab suppliers. No hydrofluoric acid.

Design principle: If a chemical is not available at a Canadian grocery store, pharmacy, hardware store, or Amazon.ca, we find an alternative process that avoids it entirely. This constraint drives every material choice in the project.

What We Eliminated

Conventional semiconductor fabrication relies on chemicals that are toxic, expensive, or restricted. Albu replaces every one of them:

Etching

ChemicalRoleReplacesSourceCost (CAD)Safety
White vinegar (5% acetic acid) Etches ZnO selectively. Does not attack egg white resist. Dilute HCl, buffered HF Grocery store $2 / 1L Food-safe. Mild odor.
KOH (potassium hydroxide) Etches SiO2 (slow, elevated temp). Anisotropic silicon etch for MEMS features. Hydrofluoric acid (HF), BOE Hardware store (drain cleaner, e.g., "Drano Crystal") $8 / 500g Caustic. Wear gloves and goggles. Causes burns on prolonged contact. Far safer than HF.
Laser ablation (not a chemical) Patterns metal contacts by vaporization — no wet etch needed FeCl3, aqua regia, HNO3/HCl metal etchants N/A (uses the CNC laser at full power) $0 Eye protection required. Fume extraction recommended for metal ablation.
Discovery: vinegar etches ZnO. Zinc oxide dissolves readily in dilute acetic acid (vinegar) at room temperature, producing zinc acetate and water. This reaction is selective — vinegar does not attack crosslinked egg white, glass, or silicon. This means we can pattern ZnO channels using egg white resist and vinegar etch with no exotic chemicals.

Contacts & Metals

ChemicalRoleReplacesSourceCost (CAD)Safety
Conductive silver paint Source/drain/gate contacts. Paint or dispense, laser-sinter for conductivity. Thermal evaporation of Al/Au, sputtered metals Amazon (MG Chemicals 842AR) $20 / pen Flammable solvent base. Use in ventilated area. Low toxicity when dry.
Copper gilding leaf Thin copper film for contacts. Transfer to substrate, laser-pattern by ablation. Sputtered or evaporated copper Amazon (art supply) $12 / booklet Non-toxic.
Laser ablation replaces metal etching. Instead of depositing metal everywhere and etching away the unwanted regions (requiring FeCl3 or similar), we deposit metal selectively (silver paint) or pattern it by ablation (copper leaf). The 5W laser vaporizes thin metal films at full power, eliminating all metal etchants from the process.

Safety Summary

The most hazardous chemicals in this project are KOH (caustic) and monoethanolamine (corrosive, amine). Both require gloves and eye protection but are available at hardware stores without restriction. Compare this to conventional fabrication, which routinely uses hydrofluoric acid (lethal), piranha solution (explosive), and POCl3 gas (toxic). The Albu chemical toolkit is safe for a well-ventilated home workspace with basic PPE (nitrile gloves, safety glasses).
