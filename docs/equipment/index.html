Equipment List

Complete bill of materials for Phase 1. Everything is available new from Amazon.ca, grocery stores, and pharmacies in Canada. No used equipment, no specialty lab suppliers.

Tools and Machines — $525 CAD

Genmitsu CNC 3018-PRO — $229.49

Purpose: XY positioning platform for laser direct-write patterning.

Key specs: 300×180×45mm work area, GRBL firmware on Arduino, 24V power supply, lead screw drive.

Why this one: Most popular hobbyist CNC in this size class. Massive community support, compatible with the Genmitsu laser modules, open-source GRBL firmware. Available on Amazon.ca with Prime shipping.

Software: LaserGRBL (free, Windows) or LightBurn ($60, cross-platform) for generating G-code from patterns. Candle (free) for manual CNC control.

Genmitsu 5W 450nm Laser Module — $116.99

Purpose: Dual-use: low power (1–5%) for egg white resist exposure, full power (50–100%) for metal ablation in Phase 2.

Key specs: 5000mW optical power, 450nm wavelength, 0.1×0.15mm compressed spot, TTL modulation, plug-and-play with 3018-PRO.

Why 450nm and not 405nm: Riboflavin (our photosensitizer) has its strongest visible absorption peak at 445–450nm. The 450nm laser sits directly on this peak. Additionally, 5W at 450nm does double duty for metal ablation in Phase 2, whereas a 500mW 405nm module would need to be replaced.

Included: Safety goggles (1 pair), protective shield with focus module, signal cables.

BEBANG YW-3006 Compound Microscope — $89.99

Purpose: Inspecting and photographing egg white resist patterns after development and staining.

Key specs: 100–2000× magnification, 4x/10x/40x achromatic objectives, WF25x eyepiece, dual LED illumination, coarse + fine focus, phone adapter included.

Practical range: 100x and 250x are the primary working magnifications. The 40x objective has limited utility for microscopy on this model (common at this price point) but will be repurposed as a laser focusing lens for Machine 2.

Included: 15 prepared slides, phone adapter, wire shutter, carrying bag.

FreeMascot OD6+ Laser Safety Goggles — $43.99

Purpose: Eye protection when operating the 5W 450nm laser.

Key specs: OD 6+ at 190–550nm (attenuates 450nm light by 1,000,000×), 35% visible light transmittance, CE EN207 certified, fits over prescription glasses.

Non-negotiable. A 5W laser causes permanent retinal damage in microseconds from a direct or reflected beam. Always wear goggles when the laser is powered, even during alignment and focus testing.

3D Printed Spin Coater — ~$30

Purpose: Depositing uniform thin films of egg white resist onto glass substrates.

Components:

  • Brushless DC motor (2212 size or equivalent) — ~$12
  • ESC (electronic speed controller, 30A) or PWM motor controller — ~$10
  • 12V power supply (or share the CNC's when not in use) — ~$0–10
  • 3D printed chuck (flat disk, ~80mm diameter, center hole for motor shaft) — ~$2 filament
  • 3D printed splash guard/enclosure

Calibration: Use a phone tachometer app with a reflective tape marker on the chuck. Map controller dial positions to 1000, 2000, 3000, 4000, and 6000 RPM. Balance is critical at high speeds — ensure the chuck is centered and the motor is mounted rigidly.

Substrate hold-down: Double-sided tape (center dot) or vacuum (drill center hole, connect to syringe or aquarium pump in reverse).

Spin Coater Build Guide

Coming soon. Detailed build instructions with STL files for the 3D printed parts, wiring diagram, and RPM calibration procedure will be published here once our spin coater is built and tested.

Design Requirements

  • Speed range: 1000–6000 RPM (continuously adjustable)
  • Spin-up time: <2 seconds to target RPM
  • Balance: <0.5mm runout at 6000 RPM
  • Chuck: accommodates 25×75mm glass slides and ≤50mm diameter wafer pieces
  • Substrate retention: vacuum or double-sided tape
  • Splash containment: raised rim or enclosed bowl to catch flung resist
