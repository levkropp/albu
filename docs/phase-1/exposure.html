Laser Exposure Parameters

Finding the optimal power and speed for crosslinking (negative) or fragmenting (positive) egg white at 450nm.

Safety: Always wear OD6+ laser safety goggles rated for 450nm when the laser is powered. Never look directly at the laser spot or its reflection, even with goggles. Ensure bystanders are also protected.

The Exposure Dose Matrix

This is the most critical experiment in Phase 1. You need to find the combination of laser power and scan speed that properly processes the egg white at 450nm — a wavelength the original paper did not use.

The dose (energy per area) is determined by:

Dose = Power × (Spot width / Feed rate) / Spot area

Higher power or slower speed = higher dose. You're mapping the dose space to find the window where crosslinking (negative) or fragmentation (positive) occurs without burning.

Test Pattern Setup

In LaserGRBL or LightBurn, create a test pattern: 6 horizontal lines, each 5mm long, spaced 2mm apart. Each line will be drawn at a different feed rate. Create one slide per power level.

Run 6 slides, one per power level. Each slide has 6 lines at different speeds. Total: 36 exposure conditions.

Note on diode linearity: Laser diodes do not scale linearly with PWM duty cycle, especially below 10-20%. At very low S values, the diode may be below lasing threshold and emit only weak LED-mode light. Start testing at S100 (10%) and work upward.

What to Look For After Development

Develop each slide in water (see Water Development), then stain and examine:

Negative tone (no glycerol)

ObservationMeaningAction
Line remains, clean edgesOptimal dose — protein crosslinkedRecord this power/speed as your baseline
Line remains but edges are ragged or brownedDose too high — charringReduce power or increase speed
Line washed awayDose too low — insufficient crosslinkingIncrease power or decrease speed
Faint line, partially washed awayNear the thresholdYou're close. Fine-tune around this parameter.

Positive tone (with glycerol, two-step exposure)

For positive tone, use a two-step process:

  1. Pre-cure: Flood expose the entire slide at low power (S50-S100) and slow speed. This mildly aggregates the film for substrate adhesion without fragmenting.
  2. Pattern exposure: Run the dose matrix lines at higher power. The high-dose exposure fragments protein in the exposed lines.
  3. Develop: Exposed (fragmented) lines wash away. Unexposed areas remain.

Expected Results

Based on the power density analysis (see 450nm Wavelength Strategy):

  • The 5W laser focused to a 100μm spot delivers ~64 kW/cm² at full power
  • At 100 mm/min, each point receives ~60ms of exposure = ~3.8 J/cm²
  • The paper required ~4.8 J/cm² at 254nm. At 450nm with riboflavin, the effective dose requirement may be 2-10x higher due to lower photosensitizer quantum yield
  • Expect the optimal window to be in the mid-power (S400-S800), slow-to-moderate speed (F50-F500) range
If nothing works: If no exposure condition produces visible crosslinking after development, the riboflavin concentration may be too low, or 450nm may not generate sufficient singlet oxygen. Try: (1) double the riboflavin concentration, (2) add a second photosensitizer like rose bengal (available as food dye), (3) order 275nm UV-C LEDs as the fallback illumination source.
