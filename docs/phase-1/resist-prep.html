Preparing Egg White Resist

How to filter, degas, and photosensitize chicken egg white into a functional photoresist for 450nm laser lithography.

Materials

  • 1 chicken egg (any grade, room temperature preferred)
  • Coffee filter (standard basket-style)
  • 2 clean glass jars
  • Mason jar + hand vacuum pump (for degassing)
  • Riboflavin (vitamin B2) tablet, 100mg
  • Food-grade glycerol (for positive tone only)

Negative Tone Resist

Start here. Negative tone is simpler — no glycerol, no precuring step. Exposed areas crosslink and remain after water development.

1. Separate the egg

Crack the egg and cleanly separate the white from the yolk. Any yolk contamination will degrade the resist quality. Collect the white in a clean glass jar.

Yield: ~20mL per egg, enough for approximately 65 slides at 300μL per slide.

2. Filter

Pour the egg white through a coffee filter into a second clean jar. Let it drip through by gravity — do not squeeze or press. This removes the thick viscous chalazae and gel-like ovomucin layer that would cause coating defects.

Do not skip filtering. The paper (Figure S2) explicitly shows that unfiltered albumen produces poor patterns. This step takes 5–10 minutes but is critical.

3. Degas and partially dehydrate

Pour the filtered albumen into the mason jar vacuum chamber. Connect the hand vacuum pump and pump until bubbles stop rising aggressively (~5 minutes). Continue pumping gently until you observe a slight cloudiness at the edges of the liquid — this is protein "phasing out" and indicates sufficient dehydration.

Stop pumping at this point. Over-dehydration will make the resist too viscous for uniform spin coating.

No vacuum pump? Let the jar sit covered in the fridge for 24 hours, then skim any surface foam. This is less controlled but workable for initial experiments.

4. Add riboflavin photosensitizer

Crush one 100mg riboflavin (B2) tablet into a fine powder. Add to the filtered albumen (~20mL). Stir gently for 2–3 minutes until dissolved. The liquid will take on a slight yellow tint.

Target concentration: ~1–5 mg/mL. Exact dosing is not critical — the exposure dose matrix (Step 3 of the protocol) will optimize for your specific concentration.

Why riboflavin: The original paper used 254nm UV where proteins absorb directly. Our 450nm laser requires a photosensitizer. Riboflavin has its strongest visible absorption peak at 445–450nm and generates singlet oxygen that crosslinks proteins. See 450nm Wavelength Strategy for details.

5. Storage

Cap the jar and refrigerate. The resist is stable for approximately 1 week. Label with the preparation date. Discard if you observe cloudiness, separation, or off-smell.

Positive Tone Resist

In positive tone, exposed areas become water-soluble and wash away. Attempt this after negative tone works.

Follow all the steps above, then add glycerol:

  1. Measure 10mL of prepared negative-tone resist (filtered, degassed, riboflavin-doped).
  2. Add 1mL of food-grade glycerol (10:1 volume ratio).
  3. Mix gently to avoid introducing bubbles.
Glycerol ratio is critical. More than 1:10 glycerol = poor substrate adhesion, film peels off during development. Less than 0.5:10 = reverts to negative tone behavior (glycerol insufficient to prevent aggregation). Stick to 1:10 for initial experiments.

Positive tone requires a two-step exposure: a low-intensity pre-cure (secures film to substrate) followed by a high-intensity patterned exposure (fragments protein in exposed areas). See Laser Exposure for details.

Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseFix
Resist is too viscous to spin coatOver-dehydrated during vacuum stepAdd distilled water dropwise until consistency is like thin syrup
Film has visible particles/chunksInsufficient filteringRe-filter through a fresh coffee filter, or use finer filter paper
Resist turns cloudy in storageBacterial growth or protein denaturationDiscard and prepare fresh. Ensure jar is clean and stored cold.
Yellow tint too dark after riboflavinExcess riboflavinNot a problem for function. If concerned, use half a tablet next time.
