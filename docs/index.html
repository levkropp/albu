Albu Documentation

Everything you need to replicate our work. Every process recipe, equipment spec, and experiment result is documented here.

Phase 1 is underway. Equipment has been ordered. Documentation covers all four project phases. Process recipes will be validated and updated with real experiment data as we progress.

Quick Start

To replicate Phase 1 (proving egg white photoresist), you need:

  1. A CNC machine with a laser — we use the Genmitsu CNC 3018-PRO with their 5W 450nm laser module. Total: $346 CAD.
  2. A spin coater — 3D printed, ~$30 in parts. Any BLDC motor + PWM controller that reaches 6000 RPM.
  3. A microscope — anything with 100x+ magnification. We use the BEBANG YW-3006 ($90 CAD).
  4. Safety goggles — OD4+ at 450nm minimum. We use FreeMascot OD6+ ($44 CAD).
  5. Consumables — eggs, glycerol, riboflavin tablets, iodine tincture, distilled water, coffee filters, isopropanol, glass slides. ~$60 CAD from grocery store and pharmacy.

Total cost to replicate Phase 1: ~$570 CAD / ~$420 USD

Project Principles

Open by default

Every recipe, parameter, failure, and success is published. No trade secrets. The goal is to make semiconductor fabrication accessible, not to gatekeep it.

Grocery store supply chain

If it's not available at a grocery store, pharmacy, hardware store, or Amazon, we find an alternative. No specialty lab suppliers. No used equipment required.

No HF, no toxic solvents

Hydrofluoric acid is the biggest barrier to home semiconductor fabrication. We replace it with KOH (drain cleaner) and laser ablation. All development uses water.

Document failures equally

A failed experiment with good data is as valuable as a successful one. We document what didn't work and why, so others don't repeat our mistakes.
