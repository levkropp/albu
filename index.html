What is Albu?

Albu is an open source project to make semiconductor fabrication accessible to anyone with a 3D printer, a CNC machine, and a trip to the grocery store.

No Toxic Chemicals

No HF. No organic solvents. No specialty photoresists. Our process uses egg whites, vinegar, rust remover, and drain cleaner. Everything from the grocery store, pharmacy, and hardware store.

No Cleanroom

No vacuum chambers. No tube furnaces. No ion implanters. A 5W laser does the doping. Egg white is the gate dielectric. Silver paint is the metal layer. Built on a desk.

Fully Open

Every process recipe, equipment design, experiment result, and failure is documented here. No patents. No paywalls. If we can make a transistor from eggs, so can you.

The Science

Based on peer-reviewed research demonstrating egg white (albumen) as a viable photoresist for micro/nanofabrication.

Negative Tone

UV/laser exposure crosslinks the proteins in egg white through beta-sheet aggregation. The exposed regions become water-insoluble. Development in plain water removes unexposed areas.

Exposure → Crosslink → Water rinse → Pattern remains

Positive Tone

Adding glycerol (10:1 ratio) inhibits aggregation and promotes chain fragmentation under exposure. Exposed regions become water-soluble and wash away during development.

Glycerol doping → Expose → Fragment → Water rinse

Key references:

  • Jiang et al. (2017) "Water-Based Photo- and Electron-Beam Lithography Using Egg White as a Resist" Adv. Mater. Interfaces, 1601223
  • Chang et al. (2011) "High-k Dielectric Gate Insulators from Albumen" Adv. Mater., 23, 4077 — egg white as gate dielectric for ZnO TFTs

Phase 1 Process Flow

Six experiments that prove egg white photoresist works at 450nm.

1

Prepare Albumen Resist

Crack egg, separate white. Filter through coffee filter to remove thick viscous layer. Vacuum degas in mason jar until slight protein phasing observed. Add crushed riboflavin tablet (~1–5 mg/mL) as photosensitizer for 450nm. For positive tone, mix 10:1 with glycerol.

Keeps 1 week refrigerated. One egg yields ~20mL = ~65 slides.
2

Spin Coat on Glass

Clean glass slide with isopropanol. Place on spin coater. Pipette ~300μL albumen onto center. Spin at target RPM for 30 seconds.

3

Exposure Dose Matrix

The critical experiment. Program CNC to draw lines at varying laser power (20–100%) and scan speed (50–2000 mm/min). This maps the exposure parameter space to find the optimal crosslinking dose at 450nm.

6 slides × 6 lines each = 36 exposure conditions tested. Riboflavin absorbs at 445–450nm, mediating energy transfer to protein crosslinking.
4

Water Development

Immerse exposed slide in distilled water for 30–60 seconds. Gently agitate. Rinse with fresh distilled water. Air dry.

Negative tone: exposed lines remain (crosslinked). Positive tone: exposed lines wash away (fragmented). If nothing happens, exposure dose is wrong — adjust and repeat.
5

Stain and Image

Dip developed slide in dilute iodine (3 drops tincture in 50mL water) for 30 seconds. Rinse gently. The remaining protein stains yellow-brown against clear glass. Image under microscope at 100–250×.

Alternative stain: methylene blue (1 drop in 50mL water). Protein stains blue. Phone adapter captures photos through eyepiece for documentation.
6

Resolution Test

Using optimal exposure parameters, pattern lines at 500μm, 200μm, 100μm, 50μm, and 25μm widths. Develop, stain, and measure feature sizes under microscope.

Expected: clean features at 100–200μm. The CNC 3018's positioning accuracy (~50–100μm) and laser spot (100–150μm) set the resolution floor, not the resist.

The Roadmap

From proving the resist to printing a CPU.

Phase 1 — Current

Prove the Photoresist

Demonstrate egg white photolithography at ≤100μm on glass using a $525 CNC + laser setup. All-water development.

Budget: $570 including consumables
Phase 2

First Transistor

ZnO thin-film transistor on glass with egg white gate dielectric. Then laser-doped silicon MOSFET. Then Cu2O p-type TFT. Build up to a ring oscillator (10 transistors).

Budget: ~$350 additional
Phase 3

Automated Chip Printer

Precision maskless direct-write lithography with machine vision alignment. Software-defined patterns at 10μm resolution. GDS in, chips out.

Budget: $3–5k
Phase 4

SERV RISC-V CPU

~6,000 transistors at 10μm feature size on a ~7mm die. A working open-source processor made from eggs, silver paint, and zinc acetate. In a garage.

Budget: TBD (crowdfunded)
